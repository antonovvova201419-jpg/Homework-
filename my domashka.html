The national Park Pripyatsky was founded in 1969. Located in southern Belarus, this unique natural complex covers 188,000 hectares of pristine wetlands and floodplain forests.

üåç Known as "The Lungs of Europe" - one of the most extensive floodplain landscapes on the continent!

The national park is home to 51 species of meat-eating and grass-eating animals:

  • Wild boar, deer, elk, doe, hare, fox
  • Muskrat, raccoon dog, beaver
  • Rare species: European bisons, European mink, European lynx, badger

You can see all of them from a safe lookout point!
